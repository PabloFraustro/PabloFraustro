(Concept) Hand rehabilitation for chronic pain

Research background

The project is based on an existing rehabilitation treatment for chronic pain and complex regional pain, in this method patients place the unaffected hand in front of a mirror where the reflection makes the illusion of normal hand movements on the affected hand. ( More info)


Design Challenge

How can we develop an environment for rehabilitation specialists to support and motivate patients with CPRS to reduce their pain sensation with the help of Mirror Visual Feedback?






The final solution involves digital recognition of affected limbs program which translates the limited movements to virtual "healthy" movements using a leap motion device. In order to support the appropiation of the movements, the patient's hand model and amount of virtual aid to translate movements can be directly modified on the program.

I started my professional career as an electronics engineer, studied 5 years on ITESO to later work for a little bit more than 1 year on internships (cellphone companies ) and 2 years on Continental Automotive as a requirements engineer. I enjoyed having the possibility to technically be able to create devices and enhance services, being in the side of a developer was very interesting since the challenges were very rewarding.

It was until later that I realized I wanted to exploit more my creativity ( or at least on another way). I was always curious to Design ( specially with digital services), so I joined UX design meetings at my hometown. I took it very seriously and started making websites and studying Usability and Service Design as a personal project,after about one year and a half I got a scolarship to study the masters degree on interaction design at Politecnico di Milano .
